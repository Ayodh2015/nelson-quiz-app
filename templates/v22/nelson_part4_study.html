Ch. 49 Neurodevelopmental and Executive Function and Dysfunction
High YieldNeuro
âŒ„
  • Executive function (EF): higher-order cognitive processes mediated by the prefrontal cortex
  • Core EF domains: Working memory (hold info in mind), Inhibitory control (suppress impulses), Cognitive flexibility (shift strategies), Planning/organization
  • EF develops throughout childhood and adolescence; not fully mature until early-mid 20s
  • EF deficits are central to: ADHD, autism spectrum disorder, intellectual disability, TBI, and many learning disabilities
  • Assessment: standardized neuropsychological testing (BRIEF questionnaire for EF at home/school, NEPSY-II battery)
  • Neurodevelopmental disorders: a broad group of conditions that impair development of the nervous system and affect personal, social, academic, or occupational functioning
EF core = "WICP" â†’ Working memory, Inhibition, Cognitive flexibility, Planning

Ch. 51 Dyslexia
High YieldLearning
âŒ„
  • Dyslexia = most common learning disability; ~5â€“15% of school-age children
  • Core deficit: phonological processing â€” difficulty mapping sounds to letters (phoneme-grapheme correspondence)
  • IQ is normal â€” dyslexia is NOT a global cognitive deficit; it is specific to reading
  • Features: slow/inaccurate decoding, poor spelling, difficulty with reading fluency; often a family history
  • Diagnosis: standardized reading tests showing performance significantly below expected for age, IQ, and education; despite adequate instruction

Ch. 54 Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC)
Language
âŒ„
  • AAC: any method that supplements or replaces natural speech for those with communication disorders
  • Types: Unaided (sign language, gestures) and Aided (picture boards, PECS, speech-generating devices, apps)
  • PECS (Picture Exchange Communication System): structured approach used in ASD; child exchanges picture cards for desired items/activities
